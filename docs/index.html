Fig. 1: Longitudinal 10mm shift in QA phantom for SGRT

Longitudinal 10mm shift in QA phantom for SGRT by tim-buck on Sketchfab

Caption: This model illustrates our in-house QA phantom in its initial state (blue), which serves as the reference for ICP tracking. Superimposed in orange is a longitudinally shifted frame (+10 mm), precisely aligned to visualize the system’s detection accuracy. To ensure a clean, artifact-free representation, the shifted point cloud was first constrained manually by a Bounding Box (ROI), that includes our phantom, and the phantom surface itself was isolated from background noise using the DBSCAN algorithm.

Fig. 2: Longitudinal 10mm Shift of the Brainlab Lola Phantom

Recorded 10mm Shift of the Brainlab Lola Phantom by tim-buck on Sketchfab

Caption: The contour shows a phantom in the form of a female torso, recorded by the Brainlab ET Surface Tracking System. We can see two raw data sets for two different timestamps: Red (76551 3D points) is the initial surface, and blue (74883 3D points) shows the surface after a shift of 10mm in -y or caudal direction of the clinical coordinate system. Both point clouds were extracted from two single frames of the ETD Surface Device.
